Big chunk of progress this week — pushed through Days 26–33 of the 100 Days of Code challenge. This stretch was all about move semantics, smart pointer deep cuts, and then lambdas.

## What I built

Days 26–27 were move semantics — rvalue references, copy vs move constructors, copy and move assignment operators. Day 28 tied it together with the Rule of 0/3/5, deep copy, and writing move semantics around a raw pointer. Day 29 went into custom deleters and a memory pool, and Day 30 was the Phase 3 milestone: a linked list built with smart pointers. Felt good to close out the memory management arc with something tangible.

Then Days 31–33 shifted into lambdas. Started with the basics — capture by value and reference, `mutable`, using lambdas as sort comparators. Day 32 got into generic lambdas, immediately-invoked lambdas, and recursive lambdas. Day 33 wrapped up with `std::function`, `std::bind`, callback systems, and partial application.

## What I learned

- Move semantics finally clicked once I wrote copy and move constructors side by side. Seeing what gets called when (and why) made the whole lvalue/rvalue thing feel less abstract.
- The linked list milestone was a good stress test for smart pointers. Ownership is obvious in toy examples but gets interesting fast once you're linking nodes together.
- Lambdas feel like coming home after spending so long in C++ — closest thing to Python's "just pass a function" mindset. `std::function` and `std::bind` are more verbose but powerful once you get the syntax down.

## Next up

- STL containers and iterators — ready to move beyond `std::vector` and actually learn the standard library properly.
- Probably due for another milestone project to consolidate lambdas and move semantics together.
