Took a detour from the crypto API stuff this week. I started a 100 Days of Code challenge focused on learning C++.

## What I built

Not a project exactly — more like structured practice. I worked through Days 2–7, covering the fundamentals:

- Types, variables, `auto`, `const`, `constexpr`
- Fizzbuzz a few different ways (for, while, range-based for, switch)
- Functions, overloading, default args, pass by value vs reference
- References and pointers
- C-style strings, `std::string`, raw arrays, `std::array`
- `std::vector` — `push_back`, `size`, capacity, growth behaviour

Basically grinding through the basics and making sure I actually understand what's happening under the hood instead of just pattern-matching from tutorials.

## What I learned

- Coming from Python, the distinction between pass by value and pass by reference feels way more important in C++. You can't just ignore it.
- Vector growth behaviour was interesting — I knew dynamic arrays existed but seeing the capacity double and understanding why that amortizes the cost made it click.
- I keep wanting to reach for Python idioms that don't exist. Range-based for loops help, but C++ just thinks about things differently.

## Next up

- Keep pushing through the 100 Days — next chunk will probably be classes and OOP basics.
- Eventually I want to circle back to the crypto project and add that front end, but for now the C++ momentum feels good and I don't want to break it.
